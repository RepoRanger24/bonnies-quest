How to Play Bonnie's Quest

  • Click around each room to examine antiques, furniture, marks, photos, books, windows, and suspicious corners.
  • Bonnie may walk to an object before inspecting it, so give her a moment to cross the floorboards.
  • Some clues go into your inventory; funny, mysterious, or false leads may only update the story or Quest Notes.
  • Some puzzles require using one clue with another object, such as pairing an inventory clue with a locked drawer or safe.
  • Quest Notes update when important clues are found, including red herrings worth remembering.
  • The Evidence Board shows how Evelyn, Hollow Oak, the music box, photographs, and symbols connect.
  • Some clues are red herrings. They sound dramatic, but Bonnie can spot when they lead away from the truth.
  • Explore every room carefully: shelves, clocks, boxes, lamps, cabinet doors, floor marks, paintings, and odd antiques can all talk back.
